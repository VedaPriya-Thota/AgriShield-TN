Expert Guidance

Treatment Guidance

Step-by-step treatment protocol for Bacterial Leaf Blight. Always consult a certified agronomist for field-level decisions.

01
Immediate Field Drainage
Drain standing water from the field within 24 hours to reduce leaf wetness and bacterial spread through irrigation channels.
02
Apply Copper Bactericide
Spray copper oxychloride (3g/L) or streptomycin sulphate at 500ppm uniformly across affected areas. Repeat after 7 days.
03
Remove Infected Plant Material
Uproot and destroy severely infected tillers immediately. Do not compost — burn or bury infected material far from the field.
04
Reduce Nitrogen Fertilisation
Stop or reduce nitrogen application during the outbreak period. Excess nitrogen increases susceptibility and speeds bacterial proliferation.
05
Monitor & Follow Up
Monitor the field every 3 days for 2 weeks. Report to local agriculture extension officer if spread is not contained after second treatment.
🛡 Prevention Tips
Use certified disease-free seed from registered suppliers before every planting season.
Plant resistant or tolerant varieties such as IR64, IR36 in high-risk zones.
Synchronise transplanting dates with neighbouring farms to break pathogen cycles.
Maintain balanced potassium and silicon fertilisation to strengthen cell walls.
Sanitise irrigation equipment and avoid field-to-field water flow during outbreaks.
Conduct routine AI-assisted field scans every 10 days during the growing season.
